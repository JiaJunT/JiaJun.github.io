Week 12

The starting of the assignment began with a very simple question: "Who was our target audience?", and we decided to target players interested in pushing their skills, which eventually led us to decide on making a bullet hell game. This is based on the fact that it is possible to make a base bullet hell experience from previous years' works, as well as the fact that the game could be a challenge to play due to the category it falls under(action) as well as it's requirement of precision due to how dense the screen may get due to the nature of t game. My personal motivation for going along with the choice of game is because I enjoy Rhythm games, such as Osu! and Bandori!, which are also a form of action games, which also has similarities in that both are played using a mix of reaction times,memorization as well as precision to successfully clear the game.

Week 13

On the second week of the assignment, I was focusing on WEB, while Hao Che made the early game mechanics as well as all the sprites(Which we eventually called the game "ShapeWars" since the game was made up of simple shapes for both enemies as well as for the player itself), with the player being a Blue Square before becoming a blue Triangle and then the current player model at the end of the game while i tossed out ideas for enemy attack patterns while playing bullet hell games, such as "Cage of bullets" on iOS. We also decided to make bullets 'Autofire' around this period because we didn't feel that there was a reason for the player to need to hold a button(originally 'Z') down to shoot.

Week 14

on week 14, I was balancing between over the shoulder coding as well as coming up with more pattern ideas, and suggesting to nerf the homing bullets, by either having them fire less frequently of reducing its' drop rate. We eventually decided to nerf the drop rate of the ability, as having 3 homing bullets made the game extremely easy.
