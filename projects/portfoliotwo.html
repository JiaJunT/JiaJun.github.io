10th Feb 2020

Closing thoughts/final statements
P2/FSD is now over, and i have taken an extra day to gater my opinion on the project. if i were to try and describe it in a sentence, it would be:
"There was no love for this project".
due to the team composition, as well as the natural lack of peer coding support, I feel that the never really got to bond into one, maybe because we lacked an extrovert that was capable of tearing down our walls, but by the end, not even the people that made the game was willing to play the game, so it was to be expected that the final product was a flop in my eyes, especially since i can't currently look back at the product and feel any sort of excitement, for that flame already got burnt out a long time ago.
what really killed any motivation for me was probably a combination of two factors as well as the final straw: after the initial hackathon presentation,where I asked if there was any feedback for "CitySaviours v1" and was only given the response of "Okay"
how i pushed for a "storyboard" before said hackathon and got a wirefrane for "map 1" and nothing else
and during th last few days when the "design" portion came in the day before the final presentation with
(1)buttons in the same layer as the background
(2)backgrounds of different sizes and colours ranging from 500px-1k+px(bright text colours would be obstructed by bright background and vice versa.)
(3)missing UI buttons
(4)some objects with hardtext(i.e. cards, menu) and some objects that are softtext(i.e. questions)
(5)a confession that the designer didn't even bother to look in the "sprites" or "background" folder to see how the images should have looked like
Is it an experience? Yes.
Do I like how it ended? No, not really.
Would I do it all over again if given the chance? If there was any form of passion in it then yes.
